Atmananda Alignment Mat

the atmananda yoga mat One of the ways in which Jhon T has revolutionized the idea behind proper body alignment in yoga asanas is by creating The Atmananda Yoga Mat. The Atmananda Yoga Mat is specifically designed to be proportionate to students' body heights. The lines and shapes on the mat have been calculated to keep the body in perfect alignment during yoga practice, and, as such, achieve the maximum benefit out of each pose.

Here's how it works:

The horizontal line located one inch from the front edge of your mat is called “The 1st Line.” When asked to stand at the the front of your mat, always maintain your toes directly behind The 1st Line. When called into positions such as Downward Facing Dog or Plank, always maintain your middle finger directly behind The 1st Line. Using The 1 st Line as a home base, the yogi is able to find mental clarity and stability via a solid starting and ending place for any sequence.

The vertical line that runs down the center of your mat is called “The Middle Line.” This line represents your spinal cord, solar plexus, navel, pubic bone, and nasal bone. The Middle Line maintains your body in perfect harmony and symmetry with the mat so that both sides of your mind and body develop evenly. The Middle Line also serves as a good reminder to maintain a straight and healthy spine throughout your yoga practice. The two lines that run approximately 8 inches vertically from the 1st line and frame the Middle Line are called The Parallel Lines. Hug the insteps of your feet to The Parallel Lines when you are practices postures like Mountain Pose or Standing Forward Bend. The Parallel Lines maintain your feet a comfortable hips' distance apart so that you can sense more stability in your yoga practice.

Working your way from the front of the mat to the back, the second horizontal line you see is called “The 2nd Line.” This line is an excellent marker to set yourself up for arm balancing postures such as Crow, Side Crow, and Peacock. For example, to practice Crow Pose, simply maintain your middle fingers in line with The 1st Line, and your toes on top of The 2nd Line. Then, place your knees on top of your triceps and balance on your hands with the feet off of the floor.

“The Diagonal Lines” and “the Triangles” that you see toward the back of your mat are useful for all positions that require your back foot to be at a 45 degree angle (Warrior 1, Pyramid, Revolved Triangle, etc.) Simply place the ball of your foot inside the triangle and then spiral your heel down toward the base of the Diagonal Line.The end result will have your foot placed perfectly on the diagonal line with your heel at the point where the Diagonal Line and the Middle Line meet. Proper use of the Triangles and the Diagonal Lines ensure injury prevention in the knees and steadiness in the hips, among other benefits. When practicing postures such as High Lunge, place your back toes inside the Circle in order to properly and safely stretch your iliopsoas (the front of your hip).

For a healthy practice of poses such as Plank and Downward Dog, always maintain your toes inside the “XBoxes,” directly in line with the 3rd Line. This practice ensures a healthy stretch of the achilles tendons, hamstrings, and calves. The 4th Line can also be used as a point of reference when you are asked to ground your back foot at a 90 degree angle (Warrior 2, Extended Side Angle Pose, etc.). Overall, proper use of these lines and boxes ensures that you do not overextend yourself in the yoga postures. Likewise, they will prevent you from cutting your own potential short. Finding this balance will facilitate a happy, healthy, and most importantly, sustainable yoga practice. Yoga Asana: a yogic position that is held firm, but relaxed, for extended periods. (Ex: downward facing dog, peacock pose, handstand, etc.)
